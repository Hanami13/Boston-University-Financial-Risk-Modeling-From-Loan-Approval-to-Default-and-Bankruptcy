# Boston-University-MS-Data-Science
Boston University: Financial Risk Modeling From Loan Approval to Default an Bankruptcy

This capstone project focuses on financial risk modeling in the finance industry, examining the risk assessment process from loan approval to default and bankruptcy. Financial institutions play a crucial role in the stability of the global economy, and many major financial crises have been associated with weaknesses in credit assessment, excessive risk-taking, and poor monitoring of financial obligations. For this reason, banks and other financial institutions must carefully evaluate borrowers and counterparties before entering into financial relationships.

The project uses real-world datasets to analyze different stages of financial risk. The Modified Loan/Application Register dataset represents the initial decision-making stage, where a financial institution decides whether to approve or deny a loan application. The Default of Credit Card Clients dataset captures the behavioral stage, focusing on repayment patterns and characteristics associated with future default. Finally, the Polish Bankruptcy dataset addresses the most severe outcome: whether a company is likely to fail financially and become unable to meet its obligations. Together, these datasets support a multi-stage predictive framework for financial risk modeling. The framework connects loan approval decisions, borrower repayment behavior, and bankruptcy prediction into one broader analytical perspective. 

The goal of the project is to identify key financial, demographic, and behavioral features that may help predict risky decisions, support credit assessment, improve portfolio allocation, and reduce potential losses
